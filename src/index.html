Making Sense of
Making Parole in
New York State

The New York State Board of Parole is the sole entity with discretionary authority to grant release to eligible individuals incarcerated with DOCCS. It is an independent body responsible for determining parole, setting parole conditions, and revoking parole when conditions have been violated.

According to DOCCS interviews with the parole board "include answering questions about prior criminal activity, custodial record, program participation, future goals and release plans."
Nowhere does it say that a certain answer or plan is the best to get you parole.
What makes someone a better candidate for parole?
Do factors such as age, race, crime, time served, facility and county of commitment play a role in predicting parole interview outcomes?
With this project, I set out to find trends in parole outcomes using only publicly available data about parole interviews between September 2021 to September 2023.

Over these two years, the Board of Parole of New York State conducted 0 interviews with persons eligible for parole.

There were a total of 9,077 individuals interviewed.

2,760 of those individuals were interviewed more than once.

Taking a look at the outcomes
of these interviews, approximately
47% were denied parole,
34% were granted parole.

The remaining interviews were either postponed, or not yet given a decision / given a decision of "other".

For our purposes, we're going to focus on the interviews with decisions of either denied or granted.

We'll look at variables of interest such as race, age, gender, interview type and how they relate to denied and granted interview decisions.
